Born A Crime Chapter 1 & 2 Summary

The book Born A Crime by Trevor Noah talks about the long history of conflict between
Zulu and Xhosa tribes, where the Zulu were warrior people who battled against European
invaders. The Xhosa people tried to learn from the Europeans using strategies instead of
violence to resist invasion.

Trevor Noah was born to a white Swiss father and a black Xhosa mother as a result of
what was considered a criminal act back then. The Xhosa women were mostly known for
being prostitutes and for Patricia, it was a scandalous case because during apartheid the
relationship between white and black was punishable for five years in jail. Trevor’s
experience growing up in Johannesburg, South Africa with his rebellious, fearless and
religious mother who threw her own son from a moving car and jumped with Trevor's
little brother who was 2 years old, saving their lives from a Zulu man whom they had
been quarreling with in a minibus.

Trevor’s mother, Patricia Nombuyiselo, was a fiercely religious woman who took her
mischievous son to three churches every sunday(the Black one, white one and mixed), a
prayer meeting on Tuesday, Bible study on Wednesday and youth church on Thursday,
even when black South Africans were cowering in their homes.

The story about apartheid times in South Africa and how they resisted is similar to the
way the Rwandan kingdom resisted Germany colonials, using strategies instead of
violence. I can’t wait to read the next chapter.
